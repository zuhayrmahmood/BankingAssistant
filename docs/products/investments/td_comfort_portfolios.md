# TD Comfort Portfolios (Mutual Funds)

Maps to `InvestmentType.MUTUAL_FUND`. `MutualFundDetails.mer` varies by tier (see note below); `units`/`nav` populate per the client's specific holding.

## What It Is
A "one-stop" fund-of-funds solution — each portfolio blends multiple underlying TD Mutual Funds across asset classes, sectors, and geographies to match a stated risk tolerance, so the client doesn't have to pick individual funds themselves.

## The Six Tiers (low risk → high risk)
| Portfolio | Risk | Objective |
|---|---|---|
| TD Comfort Conservative Income | Low | Income, with modest potential for long-term capital growth |
| TD Comfort Balanced Income | Low-Medium | Income, with some potential for long-term capital growth |
| TD Comfort Balanced | Low-Medium | Interest and income, with potential for modest long-term capital growth |
| TD Comfort Balanced Growth | Low-Medium | Long-term capital growth, with potential to earn some income |
| TD Comfort Growth | Medium | Long-term capital growth, with potential to earn some income |
| TD Comfort Aggressive Growth | Medium | Long-term capital growth (equity-heavy) |

## Minimums
- Pre-Authorized Purchase Plan: as little as **$25 per transaction**, no setup or additional fees
- Low initial investment minimums generally (exact dollar figure not published on the overview page — confirm at account opening)

## Fees
- MER not published on the product overview page — TD states MER "varies depending on the type of fund and how actively managed it is" (see `mutual_fund_fee_structures.md` for how MERs are built up). Pull the specific MER from each portfolio's Fund Facts document before quoting a number to a client.

## Who This Fits
This is the natural mutual-fund recommendation for a client who wants "someone else to manage the mix" rather than picking funds themselves — good fit for a client with idle cash beyond an emergency fund who is intimidated by direct investing. Match the tier to the client's stated risk tolerance and time horizon during the conversation; don't default to one tier.
